Adelina

“Confusion and clutter are the failure of design, not the attributes of information.”

Adelina is an application which is meant to connect the elderly with volunteering oppurtunities. The project was the result of our task to create a product which contributes to active aging and improves the quality of life for our seniors.

Research

In order to develop a product that actually speaks to the wants and habits of elderly people, we wanted to gain real insight into their lives. In order to do that we conducted a focus group interview, discussing their overall use of technology, what they do in their free time and their attitudes towards helping and being helped. It is easy to make the assumption that all elderly only need assistance, but we learned that it is hard to strike a balance between offering them real benefits and infantilizing the elderly.

Our interviews resulted in personas that strongly shaped our product concept. Instead of offering an app that assists the elderly physically only, we decided to develop a social platform which enables them to help NGOs in social and light work settings. This will provide purpose and community - very important factors in active aging.

Ideation and Prototyping

To develop the requirements of our product we wrote user stories and did extensive sketching and paper prototyping. One of the biggest challenges while thinking about the specific functionalities of the platform were the struggles of the elderly to use phones and apps. At the end we settled for a web interface for the NGOs and to use texting to communicate with the elderly most effectively. After that we digitalized and substantialized our work as wireframes in Adobe XD.

Design

The screens you see are meant for the people and NGOs who host the non-profit events.

Our goal was to create a design which conveys vigor and competence.

The calm green tones and the simple layout help you easily have an overview over all your options without being overwhelmed by the inforation.
